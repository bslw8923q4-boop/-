# Sirius Vael Character Reference

Reference image:

`Media/References/Sirius_Vael_Character_Reference.png`

## Core Read

Sirius Vael should read as a cold, charismatic noble-warrior: young, beautiful, controlled, and dangerous. He is not a bright heroic knight. He is a man with a dream who already understands that other people may become the price of it.

## Visual Pillars

- Age: 26.
- Height: tall.
- Build: athletic, lean, disciplined.
- Hair: snow-white or silver-white, soft but slightly wild.
- Eyes: pale cold blue or grey-blue.
- Armor: silver-black plate and leather, engraved but not flashy.
- Cloak: heavy black cloak with worn edges and noble silhouette.
- Symbol: eight-point star/compass motif on pendant, clasp, banner, and castle heraldry.
- Palette: charcoal black, dark iron, aged silver, cold ivory, muted blue-grey.

## Costume Notes

- The armor should feel expensive but weathered.
- The cape should make his silhouette larger and more royal from the back.
- The chest and collar area need ornate metalwork for close shots.
- The pendant is important: it can become a gameplay/story object tied to Vael's oath, family fall, or castle claim.
- Avoid golden hero fantasy. If metal needs warmth, keep it as old brass accents only.

## Personality in Animation

- Idle: still, upright, minimal wasted movement.
- Walk: calm and deliberate, not swaggering.
- Combat stance: controlled, precise, almost ceremonial.
- Victory reaction: no big celebration; a small pause or glance is enough.
- Dialogue posture: direct eye contact, head slightly down, intimidating without yelling.

## Prototype Implementation

The current UE prototype uses the Third Person character as a stand-in. Until a real Sirius skeletal mesh is imported, the prototype should signal the direction with:

- dark cloak marker attached behind the pawn;
- cool metal chest plate marker;
- star/medallion marker on the torso;
- desaturated lighting and cold UI language.

## Asset Target

Final asset should be a UE5-ready skeletal character with:

- 3rd-person combat animations;
- cape/cloth simulation or baked cape variants;
- modular armor material slots;
- face/hair close-up quality for dialogue scenes;
- pendant and cloak clasp as separate sockets or mesh parts.
